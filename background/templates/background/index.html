In various fields, decision-making often involves inherent uncertainties and risks. Monte Carlo simulation, a computational technique named after the famous casino city, Monte Carlo, offers a robust method to analyze complex systems and assess the likelihood of different outcomes. This article provides an overview of Monte Carlo simulation, its underlying principles, and how it works.

Monte Carlo simulation is a statistical technique that employs random sampling and probabilistic modeling to simulate and analyze a wide range of complex systems. It is particularly useful when dealing with situations that involve multiple variables, uncertain inputs, and interdependencies.

It finds applications in various domains, including finance, engineering, project management, manufacturing, healthcare, and more. Some examples include portfolio optimization, option pricing, reliability analysis, supply chain optimization, and resource allocation.
